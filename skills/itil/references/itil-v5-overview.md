# ITIL (Version 5) — What Changed from ITIL 4

> **As of 2026-Q1 official ITIL v5 Foundation materials (PeopleCert / Axelos)**

---

## Name and Scope Change

ITIL no longer stands for "IT Infrastructure Library." It is now described as:

> **A framework and best practice guidance for digital product and service management.**

The shift from "IT service management" to "digital product and service management" reflects the move to treating technology offerings as products with full lifecycles — not just services to be maintained reactively.

## What Stayed the Same

- **34 Practices** from ITIL 4 are retained (Incident Management, Change Enablement, Problem Management, etc.)
- **4 Dimensions** model still applies (Organizations & People, Information & Technology, Partners & Suppliers, Value Streams & Processes)
- **ITIL 4 certification** holders can upgrade via the Foundation Bridge — no full re-certification required
- Existing workflows (incident, change, problem) are 100% compatible

---

## Key Terminology Changes (v4 → v5)

| ITIL 4 Term | ITIL v5 Term | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Service Lifecycle | Digital Product Lifecycle | Broader scope including discovery, design, delivery, support |
| Service Value System | Digital Value System | Same concept, updated framing |
| ITIL 4 | ITIL (Version 5) | Official naming convention — not "ITIL 5" |
| IT Service Management | Digital Product & Service Management | Scope expansion |

## Three New Pillars

### 1. AI Governance (Unique Extension Module)
- The only extension module in the entire ITIL v5 qualification scheme
- Addresses AI adoption across the full 6C capability spectrum
- See `ai-governance-6c.md` for the complete 6C model

### 2. Sustainability
- Woven throughout the framework, not a separate add-on
- Applies to change decisions, resource allocation, and service design
- See `sustainability.md` for change-decision integration

### 3. User Experience (UX-Driven Service Management)
- Services designed from the user experience backward
- Outcome-driven metrics replace pure uptime/SLA metrics
- Aligns with SRE Error Budget concepts — see `sre-integration.md`

---

## Guiding Principles (Unchanged from ITIL 4)

1. Focus on value
2. Start where you are
3. Progress iteratively with feedback
4. Collaborate and promote visibility
5. Think and work holistically
6. Keep it simple and practical
7. Optimize and automate
